Introduction¶

This document provide an anlysis for a dataset with over 100k medical appointment in Brazil, the goal is to determine if the patient will show up to his\her appointment or not.
The focuse here is on a single dependent variable that hold the show up value.
The independent variables that might or might not effect the show up value:

  1. The patient's age.
  2. The patient's gender.
  3. The patient's health record(if she/he has chronic diseases).
  4. The SMS.
  5. The duration between the scheduled day and the appointment day.

The questions that will be analysed:

  • Is the patients' age and gender will help us to predict if they will show up for their scheduled appointment?
  • Is the patients' health record will help us to predict if they will show up for their scheduled appointment?
  • Will the patients show up for their scheduled appointment if they've recevied a SMS?
  • Is the duration between the day the patients scheduled thier appointment and the appointment day will help us to predict if they will show up to thier scheduled appointment?

Data Cleaning¶

Datatypes and null values:¶

There is no null value as info() function stated, but for the datatype:

  • PatientId convert int not float. IDs are integers normally.
  • ScheduledDay and AppintmentDay has object(string) datatype and it should be datetime datatype to analyze it easily.
In [9]:
#Convert PatientId to int64, using numpy's function astype(<datatype>):
df['PatientId'] = df['PatientId'].astype('int64')

#Convert the dates datatype to datetime, using pandas's function to_datetime():
df['ScheduledDay'] = pd.to_datetime(df['ScheduledDay'])
df['AppointmentDay'] = pd.to_datetime(df['AppointmentDay'])

Aggregating New Columns¶

DateDifference Column:¶

I think the duration between the schedule day and appointment is important, if it is long the patient might forget the appointment date.
So, I'll add new column with that difference.

In [12]:
df['DateDifference'] = (df['AppointmentDay'] - df['ScheduledDay']).dt.days

ChronicDisease Column:¶

This column has two values 0 or 1. 0 if the patient has a chronic disease (hypertension or diabetes).
They might care more about their health and go to their appointments.

In [13]:
#To check hipertension and diabetes columns, numpy's function where(condtion/s, exprestion1, exprestion2) is used:
df['ChronicDisease'] = np.where( (df['Hipertension']==1)|(df['Diabetes'] ==1), 1, 0)

Conclusions¶

After testing a set of factors to see whither the patients will show up or not with five independent varialbes (age, gender, SMS, chronic disease and date differeance), the result is only date differance will help us to predict if patient will show up or not.

Limitations:¶

The data frame dosen't have equal number of patients for each tested gruops.
